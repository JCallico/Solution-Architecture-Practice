# Architecture Forums

## Overview

Architecture Forums provide structured venues for architects, engineers, and leaders to discuss architectural topics, share knowledge, collaborate on decisions, and build community. Regular forums ensure consistent communication, gather diverse perspectives, and foster alignment across the organization.

**Forum Objectives:**
- ✓ Share architecture knowledge and patterns
- ✓ Gather diverse input on decisions
- ✓ Build community among architects and engineers
- ✓ Discuss emerging technologies and trends
- ✓ Coordinate architecture work across initiatives
- ✓ Mentor less experienced architects

## Forum Types and Schedule

### Weekly Architecture Standup

**Purpose:** Brief synchronous check-in on current initiatives, blockers, and decisions in progress

**Audience:** All architects (required), interested engineers (optional)

**Frequency:** Every Friday, 10:00-10:30 AM (30 min)

**Format:** In-person (conference room) + Slack video

**Agenda:**
1. **Opening** (2 min)
   - Facilitator introduces week's topics
   - Review of action items from previous week

2. **Round-Robin Updates** (18 min)
   - Each domain champion: 3-4 min update
     * Current initiatives
     * Blockers or issues
     * Key decisions coming up
   - Other architects/leads: Quick updates or ask questions

3. **Closing** (5 min)
   - Action items and owners
   - Announcements
   - Next week preview

4. **Optional Extended** (10 min)
   - Extended Q&A for teams interested
   - Ad-hoc problem solving

**Meeting Minutes:**
- Document in shared drive
- Action items with owners and due dates
- Key announcements

**Facilitator:** Rotates between domain champions weekly

### Bi-Weekly Architecture Forum (Deep Dive)

**Purpose:** In-depth discussion of architecture topics, patterns, and emerging technologies

**Audience:** All architects and interested engineers (20-40 people typical)

**Frequency:** Every other Friday, 2:00-3:30 PM (90 min)

**Format:** In-person meeting + Slack video/recording

**Topic Rotation:**

**Week 1: Technology & Patterns Deep Dive**
- Spotlight on architecture pattern
- New technology assessment
- Best practice discussion
- Format: Presentation (30 min) + discussion (30 min) + Q&A (30 min)

**Week 2: Decision & Design Reviews**
- Architectural decision deep dive
- System design review
- Design alternative comparison
- Format: Presentation (30 min) + feedback (45 min) + decision (15 min)

**Typical Topics:**
- Microservices decomposition patterns
- Data architecture patterns
- Security and compliance architecture
- Cloud migration strategies
- Kubernetes adoption and best practices
- API design patterns
- Observability and monitoring
- CI/CD and deployment strategies

**Facilitator:** Assigned architect / domain champion

### Weekly Architecture Review Board (ARB)

**Purpose:** Decision-making body for strategic architecture decisions (ADRs)

**Audience:** ARB members (required), supporting architects, decision-makers

**Frequency:** Weekly, Wednesday 1:00-2:30 PM (90 min)

**Format:** In-person meeting + video option

**Agenda:**
1. **Opening & Previous Decisions** (5 min)
   - Review of decisions approved in last week
   - Implementation status check

2. **Decision Reviews** (60 min)
   - Each pending decision: 15-20 min
   - Presentation of options (5 min)
   - Discussion (10 min)
   - Vote (2 min)

3. **Architecture Strategy Discussion** (20 min)
   - Strategic direction
   - Cross-cutting concerns
   - Roadmap updates

4. **Closing** (5 min)
   - Action items
   - Next week's decisions
   - Announcements

**ARB Members:**
- VP Architecture (chair)
- Principal Architect - Data Domain
- Principal Architect - Cloud Domain
- Principal Architect - Integration Domain
- Principal Architect - Security Domain

**Quorum:** 3 of 5 members required

**Voting:**
- Majority (2/3 members) required for approval
- Approve, Approve with Conditions, Defer, or Reject

### Monthly Architecture Community Meetup

**Purpose:** Build community, share wins, and foster connections across architecture practice

**Audience:** All architects, engineers interested in architecture, adjacent practices

**Frequency:** First Thursday of month, 3:00-4:00 PM (60 min)

**Format:** In-person + recording available

**Agenda:**

1. **Community Welcome** (5 min)
   - Overview of community news
   - Celebration of achievements

2. **Featured Presentation** (20 min)
   - Architect or engineer shares project/learning
   - Could be: Case study, pattern, technology, tool, team

3. **Lightning Talks** (15 min)
   - 3-5 quick updates (3 min each)
   - Short news/announcements/wins
   - Call for volunteers in advance

4. **Social Time** (15 min)
   - Q&A with presenter
   - Networking
   - Informal discussions

5. **Closing** (5 min)
   - Next month preview
   - Volunteer for next month's presentation

**Presentation Topics:**
- Case studies of successful architecture work
- Technology deep dives
- Team learnings and retrospectives
- Career development topics
- Partner/vendor sessions (quarterly)

**Owner:** Architecture Practice Manager

### Quarterly Architecture Strategy Session

**Purpose:** Longer-form strategic planning and alignment discussion

**Audience:** Architecture leadership, CTO, business unit leaders, key stakeholders

**Frequency:** Once per quarter (90-120 min)

**Format:** Off-site or extended meeting

**Agenda:**

1. **Strategic Context** (15 min)
   - Business strategy and priorities
   - Technology market trends
   - Competitive landscape

2. **Architecture Practice Review** (20 min)
   - Current initiatives status
   - Resource utilization
   - Key achievements

3. **Strategic Initiatives** (30 min)
   - Major decisions upcoming
   - Multi-quarter roadmap
   - Organizational dependencies

4. **Emerging Themes** (20 min)
   - Patterns emerging in architecture work
   - Common challenges across teams
   - Capability gaps

5. **Planning** (20 min)
   - Prioritization for next quarter
   - Resource allocation
   - Training and development needs

6. **Wrap-up** (15 min)
   - Decisions and commitments
   - Communication planning
   - Next steps

**Facilitator:** VP Architecture / CTO

### Ad-Hoc Forums

**Working Groups for Major Initiatives**

For significant initiatives (cloud migration, microservices transformation), establish working groups:

**Purpose:** Deep work on specific initiative
**Frequency:** Weekly or bi-weekly during active phase
**Duration:** 1-2 hours
**Attendees:** Core team + key stakeholders
**Output:** Decisions, plans, recommendations

**Example Working Groups:**
- Cloud Migration WG: Weekly meetings during active migration
- Microservices Transformation WG: Bi-weekly during 6-month transition
- API Modernization WG: Weekly during API platform update

## Forum Norms and Guidelines

### Participation Guidelines

**Come Prepared:**
- Review materials shared in advance
- Bring data/evidence for claims
- Prepare questions in advance

**Be Respectful:**
- Listen actively to others
- Respect different perspectives
- Challenge ideas, not people
- Use "I" statements ("I have a concern..." not "You're wrong...")

**Stay Focused:**
- Stick to agenda
- Use parking lot for off-topic items
- Keep discussions moving

**Contribute Actively:**
- Share your perspective
- Ask clarifying questions
- Offer alternative ideas
- Provide feedback

**Be Inclusive:**
- Create space for quieter voices
- Explicitly invite input
- Value diverse experiences
- No interrupting or talking over

### Time Management

- Start and end on time
- Respect people's calendars
- Timebox discussions
- Use parking lot for overflow items
- Send recordings to those who couldn't attend

### Decision Making

**Forums follow these decision processes:**

**Weekly Standup:**
- Information sharing, not decisions
- Action items assigned with consent

**Bi-Weekly Deep Dives:**
- Consensus-seeking discussions
- Decisions (if needed) made via vote with 2/3 agreement

**ARB:**
- Formal voting on ADRs
- 2/3 majority required for approval
- Clear appeals process

**Monthly Community Meetup:**
- No formal decisions
- Celebration and sharing

**Quarterly Strategy:**
- Leadership decisions
- Input from community considered

## Forum Materials and Documentation

### Before Each Forum

**Announce in Advance:**
- Forum date, time, location (1 week before)
- Topic (if known)
- Pre-reading or materials
- How to participate (in-person or remote)
- How to submit topics/questions

**Prepare Materials:**
- Agenda document
- Background reading
- Decision documents (for decisions)
- Presentation slides
- Questions to prompt discussion

### During Each Forum

**Record:**
- Attend and take notes
- Record meeting (video + transcription)
- Capture decisions and action items
- Note questions and concerns

**Engage:**
- Facilitate discussion
- Ask for diverse perspectives
- Keep notes visible (shared screen)
- Timebox discussions

### After Each Forum

**Share Outcomes:**
- Meeting minutes (within 1 business day)
- Recording and transcript (within 2 business days)
- Action items and owners
- Decisions made and rationale
- Next steps and follow-ups

**Follow Up:**
- Verify action items are tracked
- Track decision implementations
- Gather feedback on forum effectiveness
- Plan next session

**Archive:**
- Store minutes and recordings
- Link from decision documents
- Make searchable in documentation

## Specialized Forums

### Architecture Education & Mentoring Circle

**Purpose:** Develop architecture skills and mentoring culture

**Audience:** Architects at all levels, interested engineers

**Frequency:** Bi-weekly, 1 hour

**Topics:**
- Architecture fundamentals (for newer architects)
- Mentoring skills
- Technical communication
- Design thinking
- Decision-making techniques
- Career development

**Format:** Presentation (20 min) + discussion (40 min)

**Facilitator:** Principal or Senior Architect

### Technology Radar Review

**Purpose:** Assess emerging technologies for organizational fit

**Audience:** Architects, technology leads, innovation team

**Frequency:** Quarterly, 90 min

**Process:**
1. **Nominations** (in advance)
   - Any architect/engineer can nominate technology
   - Submit: Name, category, rationale

2. **Assessment** (during forum)
   - Radar categories: Adopt, Trial, Assess, Hold
   - Evaluate each nominated technology
   - Place on radar
   - Determine next steps

3. **Publication**
   - Update technology radar
   - Communicate changes
   - Share for organizational awareness

**Output:** Updated technology radar shared broadly

### Pattern and Anti-Pattern Sharing

**Purpose:** Share lessons learned and codify patterns

**Audience:** All architects and interested engineers

**Frequency:** Monthly (part of community meetup or separate)

**Format:**
- Case study: Successful pattern implementation (20 min)
- Anti-pattern: What not to do (15 min)
- Discussion: When to use, when to avoid (15 min)

**Output:**
- Pattern or anti-pattern added to pattern catalog
- Case study documented
- Implementation guidance created

## Meeting Logistics

### Calendar and Scheduling

**Recurring Forums:**
- Publish calendar 6 months in advance
- Block calendar invites for all participants
- Include dial-in info and location
- Standard times (same day/time each week)

### Participation Options

**In-Person:**
- Dedicated meeting room
- Video conferencing for remote participants
- Dial-in phone option
- Room refreshments during longer meetings

**Remote:**
- Video link (Zoom/Teams)
- Screen sharing
- Chat for questions
- Recording available after

### Accessibility

- Captions/transcription for all video meetings
- Materials shared in advance for accessible review
- Accommodations available on request
- Recordings available for async participation

## Feedback and Improvement

### Forum Feedback

**Monthly Survey:**
- Was the format helpful?
- Did the topic meet your needs?
- Would you attend again?
- What could improve?
- Any topics you'd like?

**Quarterly Review:**
- Are forums effective?
- Attendance trends
- Feedback themes
- Format changes needed
- Topics adjustment

### Forum Adjustments

**If low attendance:**
- Change day/time
- Change format
- Better topic selection
- Clearer communication

**If discussions become unproductive:**
- Bring in facilitator
- Adjust time constraints
- Better preparation/agenda
- Clearer guidelines

**If value is unclear:**
- Gather feedback
- Clarify purpose
- Adjust format
- Consider combining or retiring
