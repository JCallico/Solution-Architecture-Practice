# Office Hours

## Overview

Architecture office hours provide regular, structured access to senior architects for consultations, design reviews, pair sessions, and knowledge transfer. Office hours are a critical part of our engagement model and support continuous learning across the organization.

**Purpose:**
- Provide accessible expert guidance to teams
- Support architecture decision-making
- Transfer knowledge about patterns and practices
- Mentor junior architects
- Identify emerging issues and patterns
- Build community and relationships

## How to Get the Most from Office Hours

### Before You Attend

**Preparation (10-15 minutes):**

1. **Define Your Question**
   - What specifically are you solving?
   - What context is important?
   - What have you already tried?

2. **Gather Materials**
   - Architecture diagrams (if applicable)
   - Problem statement or use case
   - Constraints and requirements
   - Research you've done

3. **Set Expectations**
   - What do you hope to achieve?
   - How much time do you need?
   - Should this be recorded/shared?

4. **Share in Advance (specialist sessions)**
   - Add agenda to calendar invite
   - Attach diagrams or documents
   - List specific questions
   - Give host time to prepare

### During Office Hours

**Engagement Guidelines:**

1. **Be Present and Focused**
   - Full attention on discussion
   - Mute notifications
   - Don't multitask
   - Engage actively

2. **Ask Clear Questions**
   - Provide context concisely
   - Specific > vague
   - One question at a time
   - Listen fully

3. **Take Notes**
   - Capture key decisions
   - Write down action items
   - Note follow-up questions
   - Record decisions made

4. **Respect Others**
   - Don't monopolize time
   - Help other attendees
   - Be respectful of time zones
   - Share the floor

5. **Contribute to Discussion**
   - Share your expertise
   - Help other attendees
   - Offer perspective
   - Ask helpful questions

### After Office Hours

**Follow-up Actions:**

1. **Document the Discussion**
   - Send summary email if decision made
   - Capture in ADR if needed
   - Add to team documentation

2. **Share Knowledge**
   - Share insights with your team
   - Post key points in #architecture channel
   - Update documentation

3. **Report Progress**
   - Follow up on action items
   - Share outcome of recommendations
   - Close the loop for continuity

4. **Provide Feedback**
   - How helpful was the session?
   - What could be better?
   - Suggestions via survey

## Special Office Hours Types

### Design Review Office Hours

**Purpose:** Get feedback on designs before implementation

**Booking:** Book with relevant domain expert

**Preparation:**
- Architecture diagrams (C4)
- Requirements and constraints
- Decision points
- At least one design option

**Duration:** 30-60 minutes

**Output:**
- Design feedback
- Improvement suggestions
- Approval or redesign request
- Follow-up actions

### Technology Evaluation Office Hours

**Purpose:** Discuss technology selection and evaluation

**Topics:**
- Should we evaluate [Technology X]?
- Compare [Tech A] vs [Tech B]?
- Is [Technology] approved?
- When should we re-evaluate?

### Pairing Sessions

**Purpose:** Real-time collaborative architecture work

**Duration:** 1-2 hours

**Activities:**
- Design solution together
- Work through complex problems
- Knowledge transfer
- Refactor architecture

## Feedback and Improvement

### Feedback Mechanism

**Quarterly survey:**

1. Satisfaction with office hours? (1-5)
2. Which office hours do you attend most?
3. How helpful were insights? (1-5)
4. Did you implement recommendations?
5. What topics should we focus more on?
6. What topics should we focus less on?
7. Would you like different times/formats?
8. Additional feedback?

**Results:**
- Shared with team quarterly
- Topics adjusted based on feedback
- Host rotation considered
- Format changes made

### Attendance Tracking

**Metrics:**
- Attendance trends per office hour
- Most popular specialists
- Peak attendance times
- Topics discussed

**Uses:**
- Inform scheduling
- Identify training gaps
- Plan specialist hiring
- Measure engagement
