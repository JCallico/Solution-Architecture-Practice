# Roles and Responsibilities

## Overview

The Solution Architecture practice has multiple roles, each with distinct responsibilities and expectations. This document defines each role, its purpose, responsibilities, required skills, and career expectations.

**Role Hierarchy:**
```
VP Architecture (Chief Architect)
    ↓
Principal Architects (Technical Leadership)
    ↓
Senior Architects (Domain/Specialty Leadership)
    ↓
Architects (Senior Individual Contributors)
    ↓
Associate Architects (Early Career)
    ↓
Architecture Interns/Apprentices (Learning)
```

---

## VP Architecture (Chief Architect)

### Purpose

The VP Architecture leads the entire Solution Architecture practice, sets strategic direction, and ensures alignment with organizational goals.

### Responsibilities

**Strategic Leadership:**
- Define architecture strategy and vision for 3-5 years
- Align architecture with business strategy
- Set standards and governance framework
- Drive innovation and research initiatives
- Identify future technology trends

**Practice Management:**
- Manage architecture team (hiring, development, retention)
- Oversee practice budget and resources
- Plan team growth and skill development
- Approve major architectural decisions via ARB
- Represent architecture in executive forums

**Stakeholder Management:**
- Executive relationships and alignment
- Communication with business leaders
- Address escalated issues
- Sponsor major initiatives
- Speak at conferences and events

**Team Development:**
- Mentor Principal Architects
- Set career development direction
- Review performance of reports
- Approve promotions to Senior Architect level
- Foster community and culture

### Required Skills

- 15+ years of architecture/engineering experience
- Deep strategic thinking
- Executive communication
- Team leadership at scale
- Business acumen
- Technical credibility

### Time Allocation

- 30% Strategy and planning
- 25% Team management
- 25% Stakeholder engagement
- 15% Technical decision-making
- 5% Other

### Success Metrics

- Team growth and retention
- Strategic initiatives completed
- Architecture maturity improvements
- Stakeholder satisfaction
- Team satisfaction and engagement

## Principal Architect (Distinguished Technical Leader)

### Purpose

Principal Architects provide technical leadership across domains and mentor senior architects. They shape architectural direction and drive innovation.

### Responsibilities

**Technical Leadership:**
- Own architecture strategy for major domains
- Drive technology evaluation and selection
- Lead complex architecture initiatives
- Create architectural patterns and standards
- Innovation and research programs

**Mentoring:**
- Mentor 2-3 Senior Architects
- Develop next generation of leaders
- Review complex designs
- Provide guidance on career development
- Sponsor high-potential architects

**ARB Leadership:**
- Chair Architecture Review Board
- Make final decisions on major initiatives
- Escalation point for blocked decisions
- Set governance policies
- Drive process improvements

**Communication:**
- Present at architecture forums
- Speak externally (conferences, podcasts)
- Publish articles or guides
- Represent architecture in cross-functional groups
- Mentor business stakeholders

### Required Skills

- 12+ years of architecture/engineering experience
- Deep expertise in specific domains (2-3)
- Broad knowledge of other domains
- Excellent communication skills
- Mentoring and coaching ability
- Strategic thinking

### Time Allocation

- 25% Mentoring and leadership
- 25% Strategic design and architecture
- 20% ARB and governance
- 15% Complex technical problems
- 15% Communication and advocacy

### Success Metrics

- Mentees' growth and promotions
- Major initiatives completed
- Architecture decisions quality
- External recognition/speaking
- Team and peer satisfaction

## Senior Architect

### Purpose

Senior Architects lead architecture initiatives within their domain, mentor architects, and drive standards and excellence.

### Responsibilities

**Domain Leadership:**
- Own architecture for specific domain (cloud, security, data, etc.)
- Lead architecture initiatives in domain
- Define domain-specific patterns and standards
- Evaluate and recommend technologies
- Conduct design reviews

**Mentoring:**
- Mentor 1-2 Architects
- Review their work and provide feedback
- Help with career development
- Provide technical guidance
- Support learning and growth

**ARB Participation:**
- Participate in ARB meetings
- Review proposed initiatives
- Provide technical perspective
- Make recommendations
- Help make decisions

**Standards and Patterns:**
- Create domain-specific patterns
- Update domain architecture standards
- Lead best practice initiatives
- Conduct workshops and training
- Document decisions and learnings

**Engagement:**
- Lead service requests in domain
- Conduct technology evaluations
- Provide office hours consultation
- Lead architecture forums
- Mentoring and guidance

### Required Skills

- 8+ years of architecture/engineering experience
- Deep expertise in 1-2 domains
- Broad knowledge of other domains
- Communication and presentation skills
- Mentoring ability
- Problem-solving skills

### Time Allocation

- 25% Domain leadership and initiatives
- 20% Mentoring and reviewing
- 20% Service requests and consultation
- 15% Standards and patterns
- 20% Learning and development

### Success Metrics

- Initiative completion and success
- Mentees' growth
- Design quality and standards adherence
- Stakeholder satisfaction
- Peer feedback and respect

## Architect (Senior Individual Contributor)

### Purpose

Architects deliver complex architecture solutions, contribute to standards, and support team growth.

### Responsibilities

**Solution Delivery:**
- Design and deliver architecture solutions
- Lead complex architecture projects
- Manage stakeholder expectations
- Document architecture decisions
- Oversee implementation from architecture perspective

**Collaboration:**
- Work with development teams
- Conduct design reviews
- Provide technical guidance
- Pair on architecture decisions
- Support implementation teams

**Standards and Knowledge:**
- Contribute to architecture standards
- Share knowledge with team
- Document patterns and practices
- Participate in knowledge sharing forums
- Help junior architects learn

**Professional Development:**
- Continue learning and growing
- Expand expertise in domains
- Develop specialization
- Prepare for Senior Architect role
- Mentor junior architects (sometimes)

**Engagement:**
- Provide office hours consultation
- Participate in architecture forums
- Support technology evaluations
- Contribute to practice initiatives
- Speak internally or externally

### Required Skills

- 5-7 years of architecture/engineering experience
- Deep expertise in 1-2 technologies/domains
- Good communication skills
- Design and problem-solving skills
- Collaborative mindset
- Continuous learner

### Time Allocation

- 40% Solution delivery and design
- 20% Collaboration and reviews
- 15% Consultation and office hours
- 15% Professional development
- 10% Knowledge sharing and standards

### Success Metrics

- Solutions delivered successfully
- Design quality and standards adherence
- Stakeholder satisfaction
- Team feedback and collaboration
- Continuous skill development

## Associate Architect (Early Career)

### Purpose

Associate Architects support solution delivery, learn architecture practices, and develop skills for advancement to full Architect role.

### Responsibilities

**Solution Support:**
- Support architects on projects
- Design components or subsystems
- Contribute to architecture documentation
- Conduct research and analysis
- Support implementation guidance

**Learning and Development:**
- Learn architecture practices
- Study patterns and standards
- Take on increasing responsibility
- Seek mentoring
- Develop specialization area

**Team Contribution:**
- Participate in design reviews
- Contribute to standards discussions
- Help with office hours
- Attend forums and learning sessions
- Support documentation

**Professional Development:**
- Complete training programs
- Work toward certifications
- Build expertise
- Prepare for Architect role
- Expand network

### Required Skills

- 2-4 years of software engineering experience
- Good technical skills
- Strong learning ability
- Communication skills
- Collaboration and teamwork
- Attention to detail

### Time Allocation

- 50% Project support and learning
- 20% Guided design work
- 15% Training and development
- 10% Team meetings and forums
- 5% Administrative and other

### Success Metrics

- Project deliverables quality
- Learning and skill development
- Feedback from mentors
- Contribution to team
- Progress toward Architect promotion

### Responsibilities

**Support Work:**
- Research and analysis tasks
- Documentation support
- Testing and validation
- Administrative support

**Team Participation:**
- Attend forums and learning sessions
- Contribute to discussions
- Help where asked
- Learn team practices
- Build network

### Required Skills

- Strong fundamentals (computer science, engineering)
- Excellent learning ability
- Communication skills
- Attention to detail
- Problem-solving ability
- Teamwork

### Time Allocation

- 50% Learning and training
- 30% Support tasks
- 15% Forum participation and exposure
- 5% Administrative

### Success Metrics

- Learning objectives achieved
- Task completion
- Feedback from mentors
- Readiness for Associate Architect role
- Team contribution

## Specialist Roles

### Domain Architects

**Purpose:** Deep expertise in specific domain (Cloud, Security, Data, Microservices, etc.)

**Typical Path:** Senior Architect in domain specialization

**Responsibilities:**
- Architecture strategy for domain
- Lead domain initiatives
- Mentor domain architects
- Domain standards and patterns
- Technology evaluation for domain

**Typical Expertise:**
- 10+ years in specific domain
- Recognized expert in field
- Published or spoken externally
- Deep technical knowledge
- Broad business understanding

### API Standards Owner

**Purpose:** Own API architecture and standards

**Responsibilities:**
- API standards and guidelines
- API design reviews
- Technology recommendations
- API governance
- Training and consultation

### Security Architecture Lead

**Purpose:** Lead security architecture initiatives

**Responsibilities:**
- Security architecture standards
- Security design reviews
- Threat modeling and analysis
- Compliance and governance
- Security training

## Role Transitions and Career Paths

### Associate Architect → Architect

**Requirements:**
- 2-3 years as Associate Architect
- Demonstrated growth in skills
- Led at least one project
- Positive feedback from team
- Readiness for independent work

**Assessment:**
- Design and architecture ability
- Technical depth
- Communication skills
- Collaboration and teamwork
- Professionalism

### Architect → Senior Architect

**Requirements:**
- 2-3 years as Architect
- Led multiple complex projects
- Mentored other architects
- Contributed to standards
- Recognized expertise in domain

**Assessment:**
- Architecture excellence
- Leadership ability
- Domain expertise
- Mentoring effectiveness
- Strategic thinking

### Senior Architect → Principal Architect

**Requirements:**
- 3+ years as Senior Architect
- Strategic impact on practice
- Mentored multiple architects
- External recognition
- Ready for C-level collaboration

**Assessment:**
- Strategic thinking
- Leadership at scale
- Technical credibility
- Communication ability
- Vision and innovation
